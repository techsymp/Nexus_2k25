Electro Clash

Electro Clash is an engaging, team-based event designed for Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) students of all years. The event blends technical expertise with problem-solving, logical reasoning, and hands-on circuit challenges. Teams will work through progressively challenging rounds, solving puzzles, debugging circuits, and assembling components to emerge as champions.

×

Knowledge/Skills Required:

Core Electronics & Circuit Analysis
Digital Logic & Boolean Expressions
Communication Systems & Signal Processing
Debugging & Problem-Solving
Logical Reasoning & Quick Thinking
Teamwork & Coordination

Event Flow

Round 1: The ECE Lockdown (Escape Room ‚Äì Logic & Circuit Challenges)
Teams must solve a series of technical puzzles to unlock the final checkpoint. This round consists of two interactive phases that test theoretical knowledge, logical reasoning, and problem-solving skills.
Phase 1: Digital Code Cracker (MCQ & Jumbled Words Challenge)
Each team gets a sequence of locked challenges where the key to unlocking the next step lies in solving multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and jumbled technical terms.
Each correct answer provides a numeric or word-based key to progress.
Topics Covered:
Basic Electronics & Circuit Components
Digital Logic & Boolean Algebra
Communication Systems & Modulation
Example Questions:
Which type of filter allows low frequencies and blocks high frequencies?
(A) High Pass (B) Band Stop (C) Low Pass (D) Band Pass (Correct Answer: C ‚Äì Low Pass Filter)
Which modulation technique is used in FM radio?
(A) Amplitude Modulation (B) Frequency Modulation (C) Phase Modulation (D) Pulse Code Modulation (Correct Answer: B ‚Äì Frequency Modulation)

Example Jumbled Words:
TEISORSR ‚Üí Resistor
TRCNREASOM ‚Üí Transistor
ODMRLTAIOUN ‚Üí Modulation
Scoring:
Each correct answer unlocks the next challenge.
The first eight teams to complete the phase move forward.

Phase 2: Circuit Logic Puzzle (Debugging & Logical Sequencing)
Teams receive incomplete or scrambled circuit processes and must arrange them in the correct operational order.
Some steps may contain intentional errors, which teams must identify and correct.
Example Challenge:
(Correct Order for Signal Transmission: Modulation ‚Üí Transmission ‚Üí Reception ‚Üí Demodulation)
(Correct Order for Rectifier Circuit: Transformer ‚Üí Rectifier ‚Üí Filter ‚Üí Regulator)
Scoring:
Correct sequencing: +20 points
Correct debugging of errors: +10 points
‚Æû Top 4 teams advance to Round 2.

Round 2: Circuit Breakout ‚Äì Debugging & Problem-Solving
Teams must identify and correct errors in a given faulty circuit.
Phase 1: Debugging Challenge (Error Identification & Correction)
Teams receive a faulty circuit diagram and must identify incorrect components, misplaced connections, or missing elements.
They must explain the mistakes and provide the correct modifications.
Example Errors:
Incorrect Component Value: A 100Œ© resistor incorrectly placed instead of 1kŒ©.
Missing Component: A pull-down resistor missing in a transistor switch circuit.
Wiring Mistake: Incorrect grounding of an operational amplifier.
Scoring:
Each correctly identified and explained error earns points.
The top two teams proceed to the Final Round.
Round 3: The Ultimate Circuit Puzzle ‚Äì Unlock the Exit
The final teams must complete a three-layered circuit puzzle to win.
Phase 1: The Final Challenge
1. Component Matching: Identify missing components and justify their roles.
2. Logical Circuit Flow: Arrange circuit blocks in the correct operational sequence.
3. Final Debug & Correction: Solve a circuit with intentional faults and present the corrected version.
‚Æû The first team to complete all tasks correctly wins Electro Clash!
Conclusion:
Electro Clash is a fusion of technical knowledge, logic, and hands-on problem-solving in an interactive, cost-effective format. By focusing on physical, engaging challenges, the event ensures an immersive experience that enhances teamwork, quick thinking, and circuit analysis skills in a competitive yet fun setting.
